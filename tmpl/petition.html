Public safety is intertwined with a myriad of other issues including racial injustice, poverty, mental illness, and substance abuse. These problems have reached crisis proportions. We have the opportunity to redirect the ENTIRE $357M SPD budget towards a solution that builds and serves the community holistically.

We believe that the SPD is beyond reform and it's time for the residents of Seattle to re-imagine how public safety is addressed in our community. The root of the racial injustice and extreme violence in this system stems from its detachment from the community it allegedly serves. We must ensure its replacement addresses this.

We propose that the City of Seattle:

  1. Establish a citizens committee in each city council district, facilitated by the residents of that district.
  2. Residents of each district will submit proposals for a public safety agency to replace SPD, and hold a series of town halls to discuss those proposals.
  3. Each district will vote to select the proposal to represent their district.
  4. A city wide election will be held using approval voting to select the most popular district proposal.
  5. The selected proposal will be enacted. The entire SPD budget will be redirected to fund this proposal. SPD in its present form will be disbanded.
