Reimagine Public Safety

Seattle has the opportunity to evolve beyond models of traditional policing. Let's democratically reinvent a replacement agency for the SPD.

Read our Petition

Public safety is intertwined with a myriad of other issues including racial injustice, poverty, mental illness, and substance abuse. These problems have reached crisis proportions. We have the opportunity to redirect the ENTIRE $400M SPD budget towards a solution that builds and serves the community holistically.

We believe that the SPD is beyond reform and it's time for the residents of Seattle to reimagine how public safety is addressed in our community. The root of the racial injustice and extreme violence in this system stems from its detachment from the community it allegedly serves. We must ensure its replacement addresses this.

We propose that the City of Seattle:

  1. Establish a citizens assembly in each city council district, facilitated by the residents of that district.
  2. Each assembly will consider proposals for a public safety agency to replace SPD, and hear testimony from residents and experts about the relative merits of these proposals.
  3. Each assembly will deliberate to select a proposal to represent their district.
  4. The seven proposals will be voted on in a city wide election using approval voting to select the most popular option.
  5. The selected proposal will be enacted. The entire SPD budget will be redirected to fund this proposal. SPD in its present form will be disbanded.
